distant reading of digital rhetoric-related scholarly journal

This distant reading project looks at issues 23 through 27 of enculturation, a refereed journal devoted to contemporary theories of rhetoric, writing, and culture that invites submissions on rhetoric, composition, media, technology, and education. enculturation publishes scholarly articles, sonic projects, reviews, and responses.

purpose

The purpose of this project is to conduct a distant reading of the last five published issues and create a series of wordclouds. Wordclouds present "a cluster of keywords and phrases that appear most frequently in a select text or set of texts" (Mueller 2017). By analyzing frequent keywords, we can get a sense of the themes or connections that are of interest to digital rhetoric scholars and, more specifically, the academic journal enculturation.

methodology

For this project, I created individual corpuses of the last five published issues (23 through 27) of enculturation. Each issue had a corpus of articles and bibliographies. The articles corpus only contained academic articles and responses. I did not include reviews. To note, many of the articles included images and videos. I did not include video transcripts that would require clicking a link nor did I include any text inside of the image. In addition to the 10 corpuses, I created a master corpus containing the articles from issues 23 through 27. Using the online tool Voyant, I created wordclouds for each corpus and noted the five most frequent terms or items.
